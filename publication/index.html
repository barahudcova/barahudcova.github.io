Computational Hierarchy of Elementary Cellular Automata

Barbora Hudcová, Tomáš Mikolov
April 2021 ALife 2021
We study the computational capacity of elementary cellular automata by measuring how many other ECA can they emulate. The result suggest that most chaotic CA are exactly those that cannot emulate any CA. Thus, we propose a new definition of chaos related to the computing power of a dynamical system.

Classification of Discrete Dynamical Systems Based on Transients

Barbora Hudcová, Tomáš Mikolov
April 2021 ALife Journal
Generalization of the previous paper about classifying complex systems. We show the method works across various classes of discrete systems. We show the method indicates a region of systems at a phase transition and demonstrate that many hand-designed complex systems belong to this region.

Classification of Complex Systems Based on Transients

Barbora Hudcová, Tomáš Mikolov
July 2020 ALife 2020
We introduce a novel complexity metric which measures the asymptotic growth of the average computation time in cellular automata. We use it to classify CA dynamics and to automatically search for CA with complex behavior.
